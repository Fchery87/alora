## üöº Problem Space

### The Challenge
New parenthood is overwhelming. Parents must:
- Track feeding, sleep, diapers, growth, vaccines, and medications.
- Remember appointments and health tasks.
- Navigate sleep deprivation, emotional exhaustion, and social isolation.
- Coordinate care across partners, family members, or sitters.

Most existing apps:
- Focus narrowly on **baby tracking** or **pregnancy**, or
- Focus solely on **parent mental health** without acknowledging caregiving logistics.

This leaves a gap for an app that:
- Honors the **baby‚Äôs needs** *and* the **parents‚Äô wellbeing**, and
- Helps partners work as a **team**.

### How Alora Solves This
Alora addresses these gaps by:
- Providing a centralized, intuitive system for logging baby care and routines.
- Reducing the mental burden of remembering every detail.
- Offering structured, research-informed emotional support for parents.
- Enabling real-time shared visibility between partners and caregivers.

## üß© Feature Overview (MVP + Roadmap)

Alora‚Äôs features are structured in phases to prevent scope creep, with a focused **MVP for launch** and a clear **roadmap** for growth.

### üì¶ MVP Core Features (Phase 1)

#### 1. Core Parenting Trackers
- **Feeding & Pumping Logs**
  - Track breast, bottle, and pumping sessions with timestamps and quantities.
  - Quick presets for common amounts and durations.
  - Daily/weekly summaries (e.g., average feeds per day, total ounces).

- **Diaper Tracker**
  - Log wet/dirty diapers to monitor hydration and digestion.
  - Visual summary (e.g., count per day, patterns over time).

- **Sleep Tracker**
  - Start/stop timers or manual entries for naps and nighttime sleep.
  - Visual timeline of sleep to spot patterns and regressions.

- **Growth & Milestones**
  - Record weight, length/height, head circumference.
  - Attach notes or photos for milestones (e.g., first smile, first roll).

> **MVP Principle:** Focus on fast, low-friction entry and clear summaries rather than advanced analytics at first.

---

#### 2. Health & Appointment Management
- **Medication & Vaccine Log**
  - Track vaccines with dates and notes from pediatric visits.
  - Optional reminders for upcoming doses.

- **Appointment Calendar**
  - Central calendar for pediatrician visits, follow-ups, and key health tasks.
  - Sync or export to device calendar (where possible).
  - Shared visibility between caregivers.

---

#### 3. Parental Mental Health & Emotional Support (MVP)
Designed with input from local medical experts and AI-assisted drafting, then reviewed for safety and clarity.

- **Daily Emotional Check-Ins**
  - Simple mood prompts (e.g., slider or emoji-based) plus optional short reflection.
  - Non-judgmental language, focusing on how the parent feels rather than performance.

- **Gentle Daily Affirmations**
  - Short, compassionate messages that normalize struggle and celebrate effort.
  - Content reviewed/curated with perinatal mental health expertise.

- **Self-Care Nudges**
  - Time-based or pattern-based reminders such as:
    - "It‚Äôs been a while since you logged any rest. Have you had a moment to yourself today?"
    - "Don‚Äôt forget to hydrate and eat something nourishing."
  - Configurable frequency and quiet hours.

- **Quick Journal**
  - Lightweight journaling space for emotional reflections, small wins, and memorable moments.
  - Option to tag entries (e.g., "grateful", "hard day", "funny moment").

> **Safety Note:** This is not a crisis tool. The app will clearly surface guidance to local/national helplines where appropriate and clarify that it is not a substitute for professional care.

---

#### 4. Shared Parenting & Sync
- **Dual-Parent / Multi-Caregiver Access**
  - Invite partner, co-parent, or trusted caregiver with role-based permissions.
  - All users share a unified view of the baby‚Äôs data.

- **Real-Time Sync**
  - Updates to feeds, diapers, sleep, and notes appear for all connected caregivers.
  - Activity feed showing recent actions (e.g., "Alex logged a bottle feed at 2:14 PM").

> **Design Principle:** Make it easy to hand off caregiving without needing long verbal recaps.

---

#### 5. Resource Library (Foundational Version)
- Curated, bite-sized content on:
  - Newborn care basics.
  - Postpartum recovery (physical and emotional).
  - Sleep hygiene and realistic expectations.
  - Partner support and communication.
- Content developed with input from local medical experts; reviewed for clarity and safety.

> **MVP Scope:** Keep it small and curated, prioritizing quality and relevance over volume.

---

### üîÆ Phase 2+ Roadmap Features

These features are **not required for MVP**, but guide medium-term evolution.

#### A. Advanced Mental Health & Wellness
- **Trend Visualization**
  - Show mood and energy trends over weeks/months.
  - Correlate with major events (e.g., vaccinations, sleep changes).

- **Micro-Lessons (CBT-Informed)**
  - Short educational modules on topics like unhelpful thoughts, self-compassion, managing overwhelm.
  - Designed or vetted by mental health professionals.

- **Enhanced Wellness Checks**
  - Periodic, optional questionnaires aligned with validated screening tools for postpartum depression/anxiety (used carefully and ethically, not as formal diagnosis).
  - Encourage seeking professional support when scores pass certain thresholds.

---

#### B. Partner & Relationship Support
- **Context-Aware Partner Support Prompts**
  - Data-informed prompts like:
    - "Your partner logged multiple night feeds this week. Consider planning a small rest window for them tomorrow."
    - "Both of you have reported low energy lately. Maybe it‚Äôs time for a shared check-in or a quiet moment together."
  - Opt-in feature with clear settings to prevent feeling intrusive.

- **Shared Reflection Moments**
  - Guided prompts for partners to check in with each other (e.g., weekly "How are we doing?" reflections).

> **Design Caution:** These prompts should be gentle suggestions, not moralizing instructions, and should be iterated based on user feedback.

---

#### C. Soothing Sound Library (Outsourced Audio ‚Äì Phase 2)
Rather than building and managing all audio content in-house, the app will aim to **outsource or license** high-quality sound content from established providers.

- **Sound Collection (Phase 2)**
  - White noise, pink noise, womb sounds, gentle heartbeats, soft nature ambiences.
  - Designed for calm, not stimulation.

- **Safety-Oriented Implementation**
  - Built-in volume guidance and recommended safe listening ranges.
  - In-app education about safe sound use around infants.
  - Potential future integration of a dB estimation tool using microphone input, framed carefully as an estimate, not a medical-grade meter.

> **Reasoning:** Outsourcing sound content reduces development overhead and compliance risk, while still delivering value to parents. This remains a differentiator but will not delay initial MVP.

---

#### D. Community & Peer Support (Optional Future)
- Anonymous or pseudonymous discussion spaces for parents.
- Topic-based channels (e.g., "First 6 weeks", "Night feeds", "NICU parents").
- Strict moderation and clear community guidelines.

> **Note:** Community features significantly increase moderation and safety requirements and will be considered once the core experience is stable.

## üß† Content & Clinical Collaboration

### Mental Health Content Development
- Content (affirmations, check-ins, micro-lessons) is **drafted with AI assistance** and **reviewed and refined** by a team of local medical and mental health experts.
- Focus areas:
  - Postpartum mood changes.
  - Normalizing emotional ups and downs.
  - Encouraging self-compassion and rest.
  - Supporting help-seeking behaviors.

### Clinical & Ethical Guardrails
- Clear disclaimers that Alora is **not a replacement for professional care**.
- Guidance for when and how to seek help from therapists, doctors, or crisis lines.
- Sensitivity to high-risk indicators (e.g., persistent very low mood scores) with gentle, resource-oriented messaging.

## üîê Privacy, Security & Compliance (Directionally)

Given the sensitivity of both baby and parental mental health data, Alora will prioritize:

- **End-to-end encryption in transit**, and strong encryption at rest.
- Strict access controls and role-based permissions.
- Transparent privacy policy written in plain language.
- Data minimization (collect only what is necessary for features).
- Clear account deletion and data export options.

While full HIPAA-level compliance may not be required at MVP if Alora does not integrate directly with healthcare systems, the app will be **designed with future compliance in mind**, especially if clinical integrations (e.g., pediatricians or therapists) are added.
